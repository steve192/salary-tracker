Chart explanation

Actual money shows the amounts you were paid at the time. It also shows the salary you would need today to keep the same buying power {{ comparison_point }}. Use this view when you want to compare your actual salary against the inflation-adjusted salary you would need for everyday costs to feel the same.

  • Monthly salary is your regular monthly salary before bonuses.
  • Salary plus bonuses spreads time-limited bonuses across their active months and adds them to salary.
  • {{ target_label }} shows what you would have to earn to afford roughly the same things today as {{ comparison_reference }}. If your salary line is below this line, your pay has not fully kept up with inflation.

Buying power converts every salary point back to what it would have been worth {{ comparison_reference }}. This view removes inflation from the chart. A flat line means your salary kept its value; a falling line means your salary buys less than it used to.

  • Monthly salary value is your regular salary after removing inflation {{ comparison_point }}.
  • Salary plus bonuses value shows what salary and prorated bonuses are worth after removing inflation {{ comparison_point }}.
  • {{ reference_label }} is the reference line. If your salary value stays at or above this line, your pay has kept up with inflation.
